Highlighted projects

Projects selected directly from the research and industry experience in the CV, focusing on optical diagnostics, shock-tube facilities, and data-driven modeling. [file:21][file:22]

Shock tubes · ML

Rapid fuel prescreening using FTIR spectroscopy and machine learning

Time-resolved FTIR measurements of pyrolysis products for transportation-relevant fuels in shock tubes, spanning high-temperature regimes where decomposition and combustion pathways are most active. [file:22]

The project links molecular structure to observed product distributions and builds a compact ML framework that predicts engine suitability of novel fuels from their infrared molecular fingerprints. [file:22]

Laser diagnostics

Multiplexed laser diagnostic array for sub-millisecond fuel chemistry

A mid-IR diagnostic system based on an external-cavity quantum cascade laser, providing multi-wavelength speciation of key intermediates like formaldehyde at elevated temperatures and pressures. [file:22]

The array enables characterization of gasoline surrogates, real gasolines with renewables, and jet fuels in shock tubes, and supports physics-informed kinetic model development. [file:22]

Sensing · Engines

Field-deployable laser sensor for extreme engine environments

A two-color differential absorption diagnostic using interband cascade lasers and kinematic mounts for crank-angle-resolved vapor measurements of biofuel blends in race car engines. [file:22]

The system uses intensity-modulation spectroscopy to reject low-frequency noise and demonstrate robust sensing in harsh engine intake environments. [file:22]

Dual-comb · Pyrolysis

Multi-wavelength, ultra-fast diagnostics for methane pyrolysis

An interferometric dual-comb spectroscopy setup with detuned quantum cascade lasers to achieve simultaneous thermometry and speciation during methane pyrolysis at MHz rates. [file:22]

The measurements offer detailed insight into high-temperature methane chemistry relevant to hydrogen production and extreme plasma conditions. [file:22]

Optical design

Advanced optical design strategies for high-energy X-ray sources

Design and optimization of a Wolter-I stacked paraboloid–hyperboloid shell telescope architecture using mixed-mode ray tracing in Zemax for wide-field soft X-ray sensing. [file:22]

The study demonstrates how careful tolerance analysis and system-level design can extend high-energy diagnostics to compact, application-ready instruments. [file:22]

Semiconductor · ICs

Full-stack implementation and characterization of integrated circuits

Design of MOSFETs and inverters in KLayout followed by CMOS-compatible fabrication steps such as ion implantation, lithography, metallization, and etching, paired with process simulation in Sentaurus. [file:22]

Electrical characterization and parameter extraction explain non-ideal behavior by connecting deviations to specific process steps, layout decisions, and parasitic effects. [file:22]
